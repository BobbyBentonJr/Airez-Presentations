Executive Summary

The Challenge & Solution

⚠️ The Challenge
▼

Without the former CEO, we lack a shared process for company strategy, product prioritization, and quarterly planning. This creates risk of arbitrary feature decisions, misaligned execution, and inability to scale product development systematically.

✅ The Solution
▼

An 8-week intensive rebuild to establish:

  • Strategic foundation (mission, vision, values, product principles)
  • Customer understanding (personas with Jobs-to-be-Done from internal intelligence)
  • Product strategy framework (objective prioritization with MoSCoW methodology)
  • Q1 2026 roadmap (using new objective criteria and decision gates)
🎯 The Outcome
▼

By Week 8, we'll have a documented, repeatable process that prevents single-person feature push-through and ensures roadmap decisions are objective, customer-driven, and strategically aligned.

Every team member will understand how ideas become features, and every feature will have a defensible rationale tied to customer needs and business strategy.

Strategic Importance

Why This Investment Matters

🛡️ Prevents Feature Dictation
▼

Decision gates force validation before investment, catch technical risks early, ensure resource availability, align stakeholders before commitment, and create exit points for bad ideas. This transforms subjective debates into objective assessments against agreed criteria.

📈 Enables Scaling
▼

Documented process creates transparency, ensures consistency, captures institutional knowledge, enables delegation, reduces rework, and speeds onboarding. Without it, tribal knowledge controls the product and chaos follows leadership changes.

🎯 Creates Strategic Alignment
▼

Mission, vision, and values are the foundation for every strategic decision. Without them validated, teams make decisions based on personal interpretation. Product vision aligns engineering, design, and product teams around a shared destination and survives leadership changes.

💡 Drives Customer Value
▼

Jobs-to-be-Done shifts focus from "what does our product do?" to "what progress does our customer want to make?" This reveals opportunities competitors miss, prevents feature bloat, and creates objective prioritization criteria.

Weeks 1-3 Deep Dive

Strategic Foundation

Align exec team on strategic foundation, establish decision authority, stop arbitrary features

Step 1: Mission & Vision Workshop (Week 1)
▼

Why: Your mission statement is the foundation for every strategic decision. A compelling vision creates organizational momentum and attracts both talent and investment.

Deliverable: 1-page "Who We Are" document flagged as v1.0 for permanent CEO review

Step 2: Product Principles (Week 2)
▼

Why: Product principles prevent inconsistent product experiences and endless debates. They empower designers and engineers to make decisions without escalating everything.

Deliverable: 5-7 product principles with examples and conflict resolution guidance

Step 3: RACI Matrix (Week 3) ⚡
▼

Why: Unclear decision-making authority is the #1 cause of organizational friction and slow execution. With CEO departure, teams need to know who makes which decisions.

Deliverable: Company-wide RACI matrix v1.0 with escalation process

Step 4: Feature Freeze (Week 1, Ongoing)
▼

Why: Creates breathing room to build proper process without new features being pushed through the old system. Signals organizational professionalization.

Deliverable: Intake form live, freeze announced by Acting CEO, parking lot operational

Weeks 2-5 Deep Dive

Customer Understanding

Understand WHO we're building for and WHAT jobs they need done

Steps 5-6: Internal SME Interviews & Personas (Weeks 2-3)
▼

Why: Your customer-facing teams hold invaluable insights that often never reach product decisions. Internal SME interviews are faster and more cost-effective than customer interviews while providing rich, actionable insights.

Who We Interview: Sales (3-4), Customer Success (3-4), Support (2-3), Implementation team

Deliverable: 3-5 documented personas (likely: GSOC Operator, Director of Security, IT Evaluator) with supporting evidence

Step 7: Jobs-to-be-Done Framework (Weeks 3-4)
▼

Why: Features don't succeed because they're technically impressive—they succeed when they help customers make progress. JTBD is your defense against feature requests that sound good but don't serve real customer needs.

Deliverable: JTBD map for each persona + integration guide for product process

Step 8: Value Proposition Canvas (Weeks 3-4)
▼

Why: Forces explicit connection between what Airez does (features) and what customers get (value). Critical for value-based selling vs. feature checklists.

Deliverable: Value Proposition Canvas for each persona + messaging framework

Step 9: Customer Journey Mapping (Weeks 4-5)
▼

Why: Reveals friction points causing churn, handoff failures between teams, and moments of delight to amplify. Most retention problems stem from poor experiences in the first 30-90 days.

Deliverable: Customer journey map + service blueprint + prioritized gap list

Weeks 4-8 Deep Dive ⚡ Most Critical

Product Strategy & Process

Build objective framework for roadmap decisions

Step 11: Product Vision & Pillars (Weeks 4-5)
▼

Why: Without a product vision, your roadmap becomes a random collection of customer requests. The product vision becomes the continuity that survives leadership changes.

Deliverable: 1-page Product Strategy (vision + 3-5 pillars), approved by Acting CEO

Steps 12-13: Process Documentation & Decision Gates ⚡
▼

Why: This is your insurance policy against feature dictation. Decision gates transform subjective debates into objective assessments. THE SPECIFIC FIX to prevent single-person feature push-through.

5 Decision Gates: Discovery Approval → Concept Approval (KEY GATE) → Scope Approval → Design Approval → Launch Approval

Deliverable: Complete product process (8-10 pages) + decision gate framework + approval matrix

Step 15: MoSCoW Prioritization Framework (Weeks 6-7)
▼

Why: "Everything is high priority" means nothing is prioritized. This operationalizes MoSCoW to prevent CEO-style feature dictation.

Scoring Criteria: JTBD alignment, Persona impact, Strategic pillar alignment, Implementation effort, Business impact, Technical risk

Deliverable: MoSCoW framework + scoring spreadsheet + examples

Step 17: Q1 2026 Roadmap Development (Weeks 7-8)
▼

Why: This is where everything comes together—your first roadmap built with the new process. This is your proof point that you've solved the feature dictation problem.

Deliverable: Q1 roadmap with objective MoSCoW scores + capacity plan, approved by Acting CEO + CTO + CFO

Risk Management

Risks & Mitigation Strategies

⚠️ Bandwidth Constraints
Weeks 5-7: Pause non-critical sprint work. Engineering handles bug fixes only. Protect strategic planning time.
⚠️ Pushback on Freeze
Acting CEO champions this as necessary strategic reset. Clear end date (Week 8). Frame as "building RIGHT things."
⚠️ Old Habits Persist
Product Manager holds firm on process. Acting CEO reinforces in communications. Office Hours provide outlet for informal input.
⚠️ Permanent CEO Arrives Mid-Process
All work flagged "v1.0 for CEO review." Strategy is data-driven (defensible). New CEO will appreciate having foundation vs. chaos.
⚠️ Timeline Slips
Prioritize: (1) Product Strategy Process documentation, (2) Decision Gates, (3) Q1 Roadmap. Journey mapping and other items can defer to Q1.
⚠️ Internal SME Interviews Insufficient
Supplement with CRM data analysis, usage analytics, and support ticket review. If time permits, add 2-3 customer validation calls in Week 4.
Ongoing Operations

Governance After Week 8

📅 Ongoing Rhythms
▼
  • Weekly: Product Manager updates roadmap status, Product Office Hours (30 min)
  • Monthly: Roadmap review with exec team, feedback synthesis from CS/Sales/Support, OKR progress check
  • Quarterly: Goal setting, roadmap refresh for next quarter, process retrospective, strategy document review
🤝 When Permanent CEO Arrives
▼
  • Present all "v1.0" strategy documents (mission, vision, values, product strategy, personas, JTBD, Q1 roadmap)
  • 30-day strategy refresh workshop to align/adjust
  • Process remains intact (prevents return to chaos)
  • New CEO validates or refines, but doesn't rebuild from scratch
