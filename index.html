Weeks 1-3 Deep Dive

Strategic Foundation

Align exec team on strategic foundation, establish decision authority, stop arbitrary features

Steps 1-2: Mission & Vision Workshop (Week 1)
‚ñº

Why: Your mission statement is the foundation for every strategic decision. A compelling vision creates organizational momentum and attracts both talent and investment.

Deliverable: 1-page "Who We Are" document flagged as v1.0 for permanent CEO review

Step 3: Core Values & Commitments (Week 2)
‚ñº

Why: Core values are the behavioral contract between leadership and team members. Critical for hiring, performance management, and maintaining culture as you scale.

Deliverable: 4-6 core values with behavioral definitions

Step 4: Product Principles (Week 2)
‚ñº

Why: Product principles prevent inconsistent product experiences and endless debates. They empower designers and engineers to make decisions without escalating everything.

Deliverable: 5-7 product principles with examples and conflict resolution guidance

Step 5: RACI v1.0 Matrix (Week 3) ‚ö°
‚ñº

Why: Unclear decision-making authority is the #1 cause of organizational friction and slow execution. With CEO departure, teams need to know who makes which decisions.

Deliverable: Company-wide RACI matrix v1.0 with escalation process

Step 6: Feature Freeze + Intake Form (Week 1, Ongoing)
‚ñº

Why: Creates breathing room to build proper process without new features being pushed through the old system. Signals organizational professionalization.

Deliverable: Intake form live, freeze announced by Acting CEO, parking lot operational

Weeks 2-5 Deep Dive

Customer Understanding

Understand WHO we're building for and WHAT jobs they need done

Steps 7-8: Internal SME Interviews & Personas (Weeks 2-3)
‚ñº

Why: Your customer-facing teams hold invaluable insights that often never reach product decisions. Internal SME interviews are faster and more cost-effective than customer interviews while providing rich, actionable insights.

Who We Interview: Sales (3-4), Customer Success (3-4), Support (2-3), Implementation team

Deliverable: 3-5 documented personas (likely: GSOC Operator, Director of Security, IT Evaluator) with supporting evidence

Step 9: Jobs-to-be-Done Framework (Weeks 3-4)
‚ñº

Why: Features don't succeed because they're technically impressive‚Äîthey succeed when they help customers make progress. JTBD is your defense against feature requests that sound good but don't serve real customer needs.

Deliverable: JTBD map for each persona + integration guide for product process

Step 10: Value Proposition Canvas (Weeks 3-4)
‚ñº

Why: Forces explicit connection between what Airez does (features) and what customers get (value). Critical for value-based selling vs. feature checklists.

Deliverable: Value Proposition Canvas for each persona + messaging framework

Step 11: Customer Journey Mapping (Weeks 4-5)
‚ñº

Why: Reveals friction points causing churn, handoff failures between teams, and moments of delight to amplify. Most retention problems stem from poor experiences in the first 30-90 days.

Deliverable: Customer journey map + service blueprint + prioritized gap list

Step 12: Competitive Landscape Check (Week 4)
‚ñº

Why: Quick competitive context to inform roadmap decisions without getting distracted by feature parity races.

Deliverable: 1-page competitive summary (competitors, differentiators, table stakes, gaps)

Product Strategy & Process

Build objective framework for roadmap decisions aligned with business goals

Step 14: Product Vision & Pillars (Weeks 4-5)
‚ñº

Why: Without a product vision, your roadmap becomes a random collection of customer requests. The product vision becomes the continuity that survives leadership changes.

Deliverable: 1-page Product Strategy (vision + 3-5 pillars), approved by Acting CEO

Steps 15-16: Process Documentation & Decision Gates ‚ö°
‚ñº

Why: This is your insurance policy against feature dictation. Decision gates transform subjective debates into objective assessments. THE SPECIFIC FIX to prevent single-person feature push-through.

5 Decision Gates: Discovery Approval ‚Üí Concept Approval (KEY GATE) ‚Üí Scope Approval ‚Üí Design Approval ‚Üí Launch Approval

Gate Criteria include: Company goal alignment (supports Q1 business goals?)

Deliverable: Complete product process (8-10 pages) + decision gate framework + approval matrix

Step 17: Enhanced RACI v2.0 (Weeks 5-6)
‚ñº

Why: Product development has unique decision points that need specific ownership clarity beyond the general company RACI.

Deliverable: Product-specific RACI v2.0 integrated with general RACI

Step 18: MoSCoW Prioritization Framework (Weeks 6-7)
‚ñº

Why: "Everything is high priority" means nothing is prioritized. This operationalizes MoSCoW to prevent CEO-style feature dictation.

Scoring Criteria include: Company goal alignment (1-5 scale) - Ensures every feature traces to business outcomes

Deliverable: MoSCoW framework + scoring spreadsheet + examples
